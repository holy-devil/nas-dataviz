What makes an achiever?

The National Achievement Survey of 2014 conducted by NCERT provides an insight to the learning achievement of children undergoing elementary education.
We have analysed the dataset to understand how the various factors impact the learning and performance of VIIIth standard students thus surveyed.

What factors affect the marks of a student?

Various things, as enlisted in the survey, can affect the marks of a student . To find how each parameter affects the learning, the correlation factor for each parameter with the subjects' marks has been calculated and plotted in the following heatmap.

The correlation value ranges from  -1  to  +1 

A negative correlation results in a decrease in marks while positive correlation results in an increase.

What are the underachievers doing differently?

The various factors are plotted against the percentage of achievers and underachievers following them. Intensity of color shows the marks obtained by each category.

Gender seems to have no considerable impact on the marks.
Students of 13-14 years are scoring slightly higher than those of ages 11-12 (except for toppers from maths). Initial increase with age might relate to the brain development of the older kids.
But beyond a point then they would constitute a majority who have failed and have dropped down.
Subject Reading is affected negatively by having more siblings. More siblings around might not give an ideal environment for extra reading.
Performance in maths increases with more siblings. More siblings might mean they are more adept in dividing things by higher numbers.
In general average marks are only slightly affected by category for toppers and underachievers General and OBC edge out the rest of the categories in performance in all subjects among toppers and the rest.
Majority of students use computers once a week.
Among the underachievers, the most common and the better scoring students have been the ones who use computer once a week. Among toppers, the difference is only of 1.5% in marks between those who use and do not use computer.
Majority of the students were found to be using dictionary to learn. Ones who use dictionary are found on an average to score higher than those who do not. Science and Social were the only subjects where the toppers were found to be not using dictionary.
Majority of students tend to read other books which is a positive sign. It does not seem to affect the marks in any adverse way.
Students having private tuition on average scored more than those who don't.
But in toppers the difference in marks is only around 1% while 60% of students do not take private tuitions.
65% of all students watch TV daily.
Marks does not seem to be affected much by watching TV. Reading subject has the most positive impact on watching TV.
Majority of students tend to read magazines every day. And students who read magazine everyday seems to be scoring the same or more than the ones who do not.
Magazines seems to have the most positive effect on the subject Reading.
Playing does not seem to affect the marks adversely. Majority of students including of toppers tend to play most days.
75% of all students including of toppers tend to help in the household everyday. But the highest scoring toppers tend to help only once a month.
Father’s education seems to be one of the strongest factor in determining the marks of a student. Toppers of reading subject have the highest share of educated fathers giving it a higher correlation value. This is consistent with mother’s education as well.
But majority of fathers surveyed have either primary education or are illiterate.
Mother’s education just like father’s provides a strong correlation to the marks of the student. But almost 40% of the mothers surveyed were found to be illiterate.
The toppers in each subject were found to have a higher percentage of educated mothers as compared to the underachievers in the same subject. 40% of mothers were unemployed.
80% of students agreed to taking help from family members in studying or completing homeworks. Students who seek help are found to score higher on average than those who don’t.
Toppers of Maths, Science and Social subject who scored higher marks on average tend to not seek help from family members.
More than 60% of the students stay within 1km from the school.
Staying closer to school leads to better marks except for Reading whose marks increases with distance.
Having the same vernacular language as the spoken medium at school has a slight negative impact on Maths and Reading while Social and Science have slight positive relation.
Maths seems to benefit the most with getting homework everyday. Other subjects peak at either 1-2, 3-4 times a week.
More than 60% of the students stay within 1km from the school.
For most subjects staying closer to school leads to better marks except for Reading whose marks increases the farther you stay. This might be due to the fact that one gets more time to talk and reason and read during the long everyday commute.

Various factors affect the marks of a student and the quality of his/her life. Playing, watching TV and surfing the Internet does not significantly affect the marks but can prove to be the building blocks of a student’s character.

Finding a balance between studies and other activities can go a long way.
